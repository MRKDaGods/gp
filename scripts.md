The script is strong as a presentation story, but as a demo it is trying to say too much at once. The main fix is to keep the voiceover focused on the user value, while letting the visuals imply the deeper functionality. Features like adaptive search radius, exact coordinates, route ordering, stationary-time summaries, and map export are better shown as fast UI moments or final outcome cards, not explained in the main narration.

A better approach is to make the demo feel like this: problem, system, proof, impact, then optional advanced outputs. That keeps it cinematic and understandable for non-technical viewers.

**Version 1: Core Demo Script, without the optional long-dataset feature**

**Title: Athar - Seeing the City as One**

**0:00-0:15 | Hook**  
Visuals: city waking up, cameras watching the same area from different angles, people and vehicles moving.  
Voiceover:  
“A city never stops moving. Every minute, people, vehicles, and events leave traces across many cameras. But by themselves, those moments stay disconnected.”

**0:15-0:35 | Problem**  
Visuals: crowded monitoring room, many camera feeds, fast switching, fragmented evidence.  
Voiceover:  
“Today, finding one subject across a city can take time, manual effort, and guesswork. When the trail is broken, response slows down.”

**0:35-0:55 | Introduce Athar**  
Visuals: Athar title reveal, city footage blending into one connected timeline.  
Voiceover:  
“Athar is our graduation project. It turns separate camera views into one continuous understanding of movement across the city.”

**0:55-1:20 | What It Does**  
Visuals: isolated clips on one side, unified timeline and map-style flow on the other.  
Voiceover:  
“It starts with a search area, then narrows the path as new evidence appears. If the subject is found again, the search becomes more precise. If not, it expands intelligently until the next useful lead appears.”

**1:20-1:45 | Why It Matters**  
Visuals: traffic monitoring, public event flow, incident review, planning overlay.  
Voiceover:  
“That means faster follow-up, better awareness, and clearer decisions for transportation, security, and city operations.”

**1:45-2:10 | Outputs**  
Visuals: UI showing final timeline, ordered camera locations, timestamps, and route view.  
Voiceover:  
“At the end, Athar can show the full path, the order of locations, the time spent in each place, and the movement between them. It gives teams a clearer picture of what happened, where, and when.”

**2:10-2:30 | Close**  
Visuals: city skyline, Athar logo, team shots, final UI summary.  
Voiceover:  
“Athar brings disconnected views together, so cities can see clearer, react faster, and plan smarter.”

**Title: Athar - Seeing the City as One**

“A city never stops moving. Across streets, intersections, and cameras, every second creates a new piece of the story. But most of that story stays fragmented.”

“Athar turns disconnected footage into actionable city intelligence, helping teams see the path, understand the pattern, and act with more clarity.”
